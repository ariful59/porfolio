My research focuses on **interpretable machine learning for healthcare**, especially **biomedical time-series learning**, **medical imaging**, and **multimodal AI** that can be useful in practice.

## Current research

At **Malmö University**, I work as a **Research Assistant** on an EU-funded project in interpretable biomedical time-series learning. The current direction combines **prototype-based models**, **attention mechanisms**, and **clinically meaningful explanations** for seizure prediction.

## Graduate research

My graduate work in the **Master’s in Applied Data Science** at **Malmö University** centers on **ECG-based seizure prediction**, with emphasis on interpretable architectures that are easier to analyze in clinical settings.

## CREME VIP research programme

In the **CREME VIP** programme, I worked on **radar-based people counting** for noisy real-world sensing environments. The work combined **signal preprocessing**, **feature extraction**, and **machine learning** to improve counting accuracy under practical deployment constraints.

## Earlier research and research-driven projects

Earlier work includes **wireless sensor network research** that led to an **IEEE R10-HTC** publication, along with research-driven machine learning projects in **skin disease detection**, **patient health-risk prediction**, and **industrial time-series forecasting**.

## Research interests

My broader interests include **machine learning**, **biomedical AI**, **ECG/EEG time-series learning**, **medical imaging**, **multimodal learning**, **LLMs**, **generative AI**, and **interpretable ML**.
